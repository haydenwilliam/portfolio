English-Greek Transformers

NLP Translation Research Project

Back to Projects

Project Overview

This exploratory project implements a Jupyter notebook demonstration of transformer-based neural networks for English-to-Greek translation. The project showcases practical applications of NLP techniques to address the unique challenges of translating between languages with different alphabets and grammatical structures.

Research Paper: "Transformer-Based Approaches to English-Greek Translation: Challenges and Optimizations"

This project accompanies a research paper that documents the methodologies, experiments, and findings from exploring transformer models for English-to-Greek translation.

Download Research Paper

Hayden, W. and Antoniadis, A. (2024). Transformer-Based Approaches to English-Greek Translation: Challenges and Optimizations. Unpublished manuscript.

Project Components

  • Jupyter notebook implementation of transformer models for translation
  • Custom tokenization and data processing for Greek characters
  • Training and evaluation procedures with sample datasets
  • Visualization of model attention patterns and performance metrics

Technologies & Methodologies

Python Jupyter Notebook PyTorch Transformers Natural Language Processing

The project demonstrates the application of transformer architectures to neural machine translation, implementing models trained on parallel English-Greek corpora and evaluated using industry-standard metrics.

Key Challenges & Solutions

Translating between English and Greek presents several unique challenges:

Challenge Solution
Different alphabets Custom tokenization strategy with special handling for Greek characters
Complex Greek grammar with cases Expanded context window in attention mechanism
Limited parallel training data Data augmentation and transfer learning from larger language models
Word order differences Enhanced positional encoding and attention patterns

Results & Conclusions

The research demonstrated that transformer models can be effectively adapted for English-Greek translation with targeted modifications. The final model achieved BLEU scores competitive with commercial systems for this language pair, with particular improvements in handling Greek grammatical cases.

Future work includes exploring larger models, incorporating linguistic features specific to Greek, and multi-task learning approaches combining translation with other NLP tasks.
